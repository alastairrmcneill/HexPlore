Answers to common questions

HexPlore turns your camera roll into a world coverage map — no accounts, no uploads, no fuss. If something isn't working, you'll likely find the answer below.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does HexPlore work?

HexPlore reads the GPS coordinates embedded in your photos and maps each one onto a global hexagonal grid made up of roughly 50 km² cells. Each unique hex you've photographed in gets filled in on the map, and we calculate what percentage of the world's land area you've covered.

Are my photos or location data uploaded anywhere?

No. All processing happens entirely on your device. Your photos, their contents, and their GPS coordinates never leave your iPhone. HexPlore has no server and no cloud component.

Why does HexPlore need access to my photo library?

HexPlore reads the GPS metadata (EXIF data) embedded in your photos to figure out where each photo was taken. It does not upload, copy, or display your photos to anyone else. You can revoke access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos.

Some of my photos don't have location data — why?

Photos only contain GPS data if Location Services were enabled for your camera app when the photo was taken. If you had location off, those photos won't contribute to your map. You can check or enable this in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Camera.

I visited a place but it's not showing up on my map. What's wrong?

HexPlore can only map places where you have geotagged photos. If you didn't take a photo (or location was off), that cell won't appear automatically. You can tap any unvisited hex on the map and use "Mark as visited" to add it manually.

How big is each hexagon?

Each hex covers approximately 1,700 km² — roughly a 50 km × 50 km area. HexPlore uses the H3 geospatial grid at resolution 4, which divides the entire Earth's land into 74,942 cells. Your world coverage percentage is calculated against that total.

How is the world coverage percentage calculated?

We count how many of the 74,942 land-covering H3 hexagons at resolution 4 contain at least one of your geotagged photos (or manual marks), then divide by 74,942 and express it as a percentage. Ocean cells are excluded.

Can I change the colour of the hexagons?

Yes. Go to the Settings tab and tap Accent Colour. You can choose from six preset swatches (coral, teal, burnt orange, indigo, sage, slate) — the whole app updates immediately.

How do I delete all my data?

Delete the app. HexPlore stores everything (your hex list, photo counts, and dates) in a local SQLite database on your device. Deleting the app removes all of it permanently. There is no server-side data to erase.

Does HexPlore work on Android?

Not yet — HexPlore is currently iOS only. Android support may come in a future release.

Does HexPlore track me or show ads?

HexPlore collects anonymous, non-personal usage events (e.g. "opened the stats screen") via PostHog to help us improve the app. These events contain no photos, no location data, and no identifying information. There are no ads. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
